Live sellers churn analysis

Each row represents the retail week a seller first streamed on live in 2026. Columns represent weeks since that first stream (W4, W5, W6…), showing 3-week churn — the share of sellers in that cohort who did not go live in the three weeks before that point (or the count of churned sellers if you toggle Counts).

What each row means
Each row is one group of sellers who all went live for the first time in the same week of 2026. For example, row 26RW01 (Jan 4 – Jan 10, 2026) includes every seller whose very first live stream was in retail week 1. Use the filters below to focus on a country or sub category.

When is a seller “churned”?
A seller is churned if they have not gone live for 3 weeks in a row. To check a week, we look back at the 3 weeks just before it.
• Week 4 (W4): did they stream in weeks 1, 2, or 3? If not → churned.
• Week 5 (W5): did they stream in weeks 2, 3, or 4? If not → churned.
• Week 23 (W23): did they stream in weeks 20, 21, or 22? If not → churned.
• And so on for each later week.
If a seller comes back and streams again, they are no longer counted as churned for that week.

How to read the table
• Sellers First Streamed on Live — how many sellers started in that row (the starting group size).
• W4, W5, W6… — of those starters, how many had churned by that week (shown as Churn % by default).
• Green = low churn (most sellers still active). Red = high churn (many sellers dropped off).
• Toggle Counts to see the number of churned sellers instead of the percentage.

Summary cards at the top
• Total sellers — count of sellers who first streamed on live in 2026 (after filters), summed across all cohort rows.
• New seller churn — at W4, weighted share of starters who did not go live in weeks 1, 2, or 3. Any stream in W1, W2, or W3 counts as retained.
• Growing seller churn — at W10, weighted share of starters who did not go live in weeks 7, 8, or 9. Any stream in W7, W8, or W9 counts as retained.
• Mature seller churn — at W16, weighted share of starters who did not go live in weeks 13, 14, or 15. Any stream in W13, W14, or W15 counts as retained.

Data: eBay Live streams, 2026 retail weeks 1–20. Rows 26RW21–26RW24 are hidden because those sellers have not been live long enough to measure fairly.
